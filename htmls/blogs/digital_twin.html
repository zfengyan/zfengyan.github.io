digital twin

This article elucidates the diverse applications of digital twins, empowering enhanced decision-making processes. Digital twins serve as instrumental tools for conducting efficient research across various domains, encompassing not only engineering systems but also personalized products. Nevertheless, future development faces certain challenges, with the foremost being the acquisition and processing of sensing data. Nowadays producers can deliver more cost-effective and lightweight sensors, facilitating easier installation and setup. Mpdern computers possess the requisite processing power, even enabling real-time data visualization. The ultimate objective of the digital twin is to simulate the complexities of intricate systems, facilitating in-depth analysis and comprehension. While establishing digital twins for smaller systems, such as residences, has become feasible, extending this capability to larger systems, such as megacities, nations, or entire planets, remains an ongoing pursuit of perfection.
